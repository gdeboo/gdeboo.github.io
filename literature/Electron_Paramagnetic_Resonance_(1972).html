3.5. Instantaneous Diffusion

At this point, we interrupt this discussion of phase memory and of the form of the echo envelope to draw attention to an effect which is related to the pulsing conditions rather than to the intrinsic properties of the sample. This is the ''instantaneous diffusion'' mentioned by Klauder and Anderson.24,*It results from the abrupt change in local fields caused by spin reorientation during pulse II and might perhaps be more appropriately classified with the mechanisms described in Section 6.4. We introduce it here, however, since it is primarily an experimental problem arising the study of envelope decay functions. It can, if overlooked, lead to a major error in the determination of the phase memory.
